<!-- ANSWER-FIRST SUMMARY BOX -->
    <p>Taking on your first employee is a big step — and it comes with a short list of things the law simply requires you to do. The good news: there are only a handful, they happen in a sensible order, and none of them are as hard as they sound. This guide walks you through all of it in plain English, with the exact law behind each step so you can see it's real.</p>

    <!-- Q1 -->
    <h2 id="q1">Are you actually an employer?</h2>
    <p class="ar-answer-lead">Yes — the moment someone works for you under your control, in return for pay, you're their employer in the eyes of the law.</p>
    <p>Before anything else, you need to be sure of one thing: is the person you're taking on an <strong>employee</strong>, a <strong>worker</strong>, or genuinely <strong>self-employed</strong>? It matters because each one gets a different set of rights and hands you a different set of duties. Call someone self-employed when the law says they're an employee, and years later you can owe back-tax, holiday pay and a penalty.</p>
    <p>Three questions decide it: must they do the work themselves, do you control how and when it's done, and is there a two-way promise of ongoing work? If the answer to those is yes, you almost certainly have an employee.</p>

    <div class="ar-callout">
      <div class="ar-callout__head"><span class="fe-icon">gavel</span> The law on this</div>
      <p>Employment law splits working people into employees and workers, and an employee works under a contract of employment — which carries the fullest set of rights, from unfair-dismissal protection to statutory notice.</p>
      <span class="mk-receipt" data-open="false">
        <button class="fe-receipt" aria-expanded="false"><span class="fe-icon" style="font-size:14px">gavel</span> ERA 1996 s.230</button>
        <span class="mk-receipt__pop" role="dialog">
          <span class="mk-receipt__pop-ref">Employment Rights Act 1996, section 230</span>
          <p class="mk-receipt__pop-plain">This is the section that defines who counts as an "employee" and who counts as a "worker". An employee works under a contract of employment and gets the strongest protections.</p>
          <a href="#">Read the official guidance <span class="fe-icon">open_in_new</span></a>
        </span>
      </span>
    </div>

    <!-- Q2 -->
    <h2 id="q2">Register for PAYE with HMRC — before their first payday</h2>
    <p class="ar-answer-lead">You must register as an employer with HMRC before the first time you pay your new employee — and no more than two months in advance.</p>
    <p>PAYE (Pay As You Earn) is how you collect Income Tax and National Insurance from your employee's wages and pass it to HMRC. You register online, usually get your employer reference within about five working days, and then run payroll each time you pay them.</p>
    <p>This is also where the true cost of employing someone starts to show. On top of the wage you pay <strong>employer's National Insurance</strong> at <span class="ar-rate"><b>{{NI_RATE}}%</b></span> on earnings above the {{NI_THRESHOLD}} secondary threshold — though the <strong>Employment Allowance</strong> of up to <span class="ar-rate"><b>{{NI_ALLOWANCE}}</b></span> a year usually wipes that out entirely for your first employee.</p>
    <span class="ar-config"><span class="fe-icon">verified</span> Rates current as of config v2026.2</span>

    <div class="ar-callout">
      <div class="ar-callout__head"><span class="fe-icon">balance</span> The law on this</div>
      <p>As an employer you're required to operate PAYE and report each employee's pay and deductions to HMRC on or before the day you pay them — this is the Real Time Information (RTI) duty.</p>
      <span class="mk-receipt" data-open="false">
        <button class="fe-receipt" aria-expanded="false"><span class="fe-icon" style="font-size:14px">balance</span> PAYE Regs 2003</button>
        <span class="mk-receipt__pop" role="dialog">
          <span class="mk-receipt__pop-ref">Income Tax (PAYE) Regulations 2003</span>
          <p class="mk-receipt__pop-plain">These rules require you to deduct tax and National Insurance from wages and report them to HMRC in real time — every payday, not once a year.</p>
          <a href="#">Read the official guidance <span class="fe-icon">open_in_new</span></a>
        </span>
      </span>
    </div>

    <!-- Q3 -->
    <h2 id="q3">What must the employment contract say?</h2>
    <p class="ar-answer-lead">By law you must give a written statement of the main employment terms on or before the employee's first day.</p>
    <p>People often think a contract is a nice-to-have you can sort out later. It isn't. The law requires a <strong>written statement of particulars</strong> — the main terms of the job — and it has to be in the employee's hands on or before day one. At a minimum it must include:</p>
    <ul class="ar-list">
      <li><span class="fe-icon fe-icon--fill">check_circle</span><span>The <strong>names</strong> of you and the employee, and the start date.</span></li>
      <li><span class="fe-icon fe-icon--fill">check_circle</span><span>The <strong>pay</strong>, how often it's paid, and the hours they'll work.</span></li>
      <li><span class="fe-icon fe-icon--fill">check_circle</span><span><strong>Holiday</strong> entitlement, sick pay and any notice periods.</span></li>
      <li><span class="fe-icon fe-icon--fill">check_circle</span><span>The <strong>job title</strong> or a brief description of the work, and where it's done.</span></li>
    </ul>

    <div class="ar-callout">
      <div class="ar-callout__head"><span class="fe-icon">description</span> The law on this</div>
      <p>Every employee and worker is entitled to a written statement of their main terms on or before the first day of work — it's a day-one right, not something you can leave for later.</p>
      <span class="mk-receipt" data-open="false">
        <button class="fe-receipt" aria-expanded="false"><span class="fe-icon" style="font-size:14px">gavel</span> ERA 1996 s.1</button>
        <span class="mk-receipt__pop" role="dialog">
          <span class="mk-receipt__pop-ref">Employment Rights Act 1996, section 1</span>
          <p class="mk-receipt__pop-plain">This section lists exactly what a written statement of employment particulars must contain, and requires it to be given on or before the first day of employment.</p>
          <a href="#">Read the official guidance <span class="fe-icon">open_in_new</span></a>
        </span>
      </span>
    </div>

    <!-- Q4 -->
    <h2 id="q4">Checking their right to work</h2>
    <p class="ar-answer-lead">You must check every new employee has the legal right to work in the UK before their first day — and keep the evidence.</p>
    <p>This one is not optional and there's no small-employer exemption. Before someone starts, you check that they're allowed to work in the UK — either online through the Home Office service, or by looking at original documents and keeping a clear, dated copy. Do the check properly and you have a <strong>statutory excuse</strong>: your legal defence if it later turns out they didn't have the right to work.</p>

    <div class="ar-callout">
      <div class="ar-callout__head"><span class="fe-icon">front_hand</span> The law on this</div>
      <p>Employing someone who doesn't have the right to work is a civil penalty offence — currently up to {{RTW_PENALTY}} per illegal worker for a first breach. A correct check done before they start is your defence.</p>
      <span class="mk-receipt" data-open="false">
        <button class="fe-receipt" aria-expanded="false"><span class="fe-icon" style="font-size:14px">gavel</span> IANA 2006 s.15</button>
        <span class="mk-receipt__pop" role="dialog">
          <span class="mk-receipt__pop-ref">Immigration, Asylum and Nationality Act 2006, section 15</span>
          <p class="mk-receipt__pop-plain">This makes it a civil offence to employ someone who doesn't have permission to work. Doing a proper right-to-work check before they start gives you a legal excuse against the penalty.</p>
          <a href="#">Read the official guidance <span class="fe-icon">open_in_new</span></a>
        </span>
      </span>
    </div>

    <!-- Q5 -->
    <h2 id="q5">Pension, insurance and paying them right</h2>
    <p class="ar-answer-lead">You'll likely need to enrol them in a workplace pension, you must hold employer's liability insurance, and you must pay at least the National Living Wage.</p>
    <p><strong>Workplace pension.</strong> If your employee is aged 22 to State Pension age and earns over {{AE_TRIGGER}} a year, you must automatically enrol them and pay in at least <span class="ar-rate"><b>3%</b></span> of their qualifying earnings.</p>
    <p><strong>Employer's liability insurance.</strong> You're legally required to hold at least {{EL_COVER}} of cover from the day your first employee starts. Not having it can cost you <span class="ar-rate"><b>{{EL_PENALTY_DAY}}</b></span> for every day you're uninsured.</p>
    <p><strong>Minimum pay.</strong> Anyone aged 21 or over must be paid at least the National Living Wage — <span class="ar-rate"><b>{{NLW}} an hour from 1 April 2026</b></span>. Paying a penny less is a criminal offence and HMRC names employers who do.</p>
    <span class="ar-config"><span class="fe-icon">verified</span> Rates current as of config v2026.2 · 1 Jul 2026</span>

    <div class="ar-callout">
      <div class="ar-callout__head"><span class="fe-icon">savings</span> The law on this</div>
      <p>Automatic enrolment duties apply to almost every employer with at least one member of staff — you must assess your employee and enrol those who qualify, then keep paying in.</p>
      <span class="mk-receipt" data-open="false">
        <button class="fe-receipt" aria-expanded="false"><span class="fe-icon" style="font-size:14px">balance</span> Pensions Act 2008</button>
        <span class="mk-receipt__pop" role="dialog">
          <span class="mk-receipt__pop-ref">Pensions Act 2008, Part 1</span>
          <p class="mk-receipt__pop-plain">This introduced automatic enrolment: employers must put qualifying staff into a workplace pension and contribute to it. It applies even if you only employ one person.</p>
          <a href="#">Read the official guidance <span class="fe-icon">open_in_new</span></a>
        </span>
      </span>
    </div>

    <h2 id="q6">The full 13-point checklist</h2>
    <p class="ar-answer-lead">Here's every legal step, in the order you'll actually do them.</p>
    <ul class="ar-list">
      <li><span class="fe-icon fe-icon--fill">check_circle</span><span><strong>Confirm employment status</strong> — employee, worker or self-employed.</span></li>
      <li><span class="fe-icon fe-icon--fill">check_circle</span><span><strong>Register as an employer</strong> with HMRC for PAYE.</span></li>
      <li><span class="fe-icon fe-icon--fill">check_circle</span><span><strong>Set up payroll</strong> — software or a payroll provider.</span></li>
      <li><span class="fe-icon fe-icon--fill">check_circle</span><span><strong>Agree the pay</strong> — at or above the National Living Wage.</span></li>
      <li><span class="fe-icon fe-icon--fill">check_circle</span><span><strong>Write the contract</strong> — the written statement of particulars.</span></li>
      <li><span class="fe-icon fe-icon--fill">check_circle</span><span><strong>Give it by day one</strong> — on or before their first working day.</span></li>
      <li><span class="fe-icon fe-icon--fill">check_circle</span><span><strong>Check right to work</strong> — before they start, evidence kept.</span></li>
      <li><span class="fe-icon fe-icon--fill">check_circle</span><span><strong>Assess for a pension</strong> — auto-enrol if they qualify.</span></li>
      <li><span class="fe-icon fe-icon--fill">check_circle</span><span><strong>Buy employer's liability insurance</strong> — at least {{EL_COVER}}.</span></li>
      <li><span class="fe-icon fe-icon--fill">check_circle</span><span><strong>Tell HMRC each payday</strong> — real-time reporting.</span></li>
      <li><span class="fe-icon fe-icon--fill">check_circle</span><span><strong>Provide payslips</strong> — itemised, every time you pay.</span></li>
