No — you cannot employ someone without employers' liability insurance. The law requires it from the first day your first employee starts work, even if they are part-time, temporary, or an apprentice. It protects you if an employee is hurt or becomes ill because of their work, and it protects them too.

Your policy must come from an authorised insurer and meet the legal minimum level of cover — your dashboard shows the current figure and tracks your policy's expiry date. You must keep your certificate of insurance and be able to show it to inspectors; there is a separate penalty for not displaying or producing it.

Being uninsured is one of the most expensive compliance failures there is: you can be fined for every single day you employ someone without cover. The good news is it's usually one of the easiest duties to meet — most business insurance packages include it, and a broker or comparison site can arrange cover the same day.

There is a narrow family exemption: if your business is not a limited company and you only employ close family members, you may not need it. But if you've incorporated — even as a one-person company employing a relative — the exemption falls away. When in doubt, insure; the HSE guidance linked above sets out the exemptions in full.
